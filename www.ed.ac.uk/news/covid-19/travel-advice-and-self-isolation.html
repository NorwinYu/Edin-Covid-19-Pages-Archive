Travel advice and self-isolation

Advice and support on travel and self-isolation including business travel and travel insurance.

Travel advice

Travellers entering the UK (from specified countries)

The UK Government has withdrawn (on 13th March 2020) the advice  which was previously available for those travellers entering the UK from a list of specified countries (which were split into category 1 and 2).

The advice has been replaced with advice for people with symptoms of Covid-19 to self-isolate for 7 days.

Specific guidance on what actions you should take if you are advised to self-isolate can be found in the staff or current students sections of this website

  • Staff – including who to contact, working from home and pay arrangements
  • Current students – including who to contact, break in your study and other support available if you are concerned. ​​​​​​

 

International travel on University Business

The University has now banned all non essential international travel with immediate effect from 13 March 2020 for a period of 2 months.

All requests to travel internationally for essential work or study must be accompanied by a both a justification and a careful risk assessment, and authorised by your relevant Head of School or Department. This is with immediate effect.

“Essential Travel” does not include field trips and international placements. If you are due to undertake a field trip or international placement before 31 May 2020, we advise that you do not proceed with the visit.

International travel, field trips and placements that are planned to start within the next 3-4 months should be kept under close review.

Please note that this does not mean we are seeking to recall students who are currently overseas on placements. We will continue to risk assess and make decisions on recall on a case by case basis.

Alternative learning arrangements

Schools are asked to consider carefully what alternative learning arrangements can be put in place to support students affected to achieve the required learning outcomes for their course, including discussing with professional bodies their requirements for accredited programmes.

Travelling on University business to mainland China, Hong Kong, Macao and South Korea

The University made the decision to recall all staff and students from mainland China, Hong Kong and Macao and currently no travel is permitted to these areas on University business. This was communicated to all Heads of School by Gavin Douglas on 30 January 2020.  The communication from Gavin Douglas on 25 February 2020 confirmed that University travel to South Korea is also now prohibited. Please contact your Head of School if you have any questions.

Future travel for personal reasons (for example to visit family or friends) to specified areas

In response to coronavirus measures the UK Foreign and Commonwealth Office (FCO) currently advises against all, and all but essential travel to some countries, cities and regions. You must check the travel advice to the country you are travelling to.

If you decide to proceed to countries, cities or regions against FCO advice, be aware of the travel restrictions being imposed that may impede your return to the UK. 

The current advice for people with symptoms of Covid-19 is to self-isolate for 7 days.

Specific guidance on what actions you should take if you are advised to self-isolate can be found in the staff or current students sections of this website

  • Staff – including who to contact, working from home and pay arrangements
  • Current students – including who to contact, break in your study and other support available if you are concerned. ​​​​​​

If you are in affected areas and have concerns about travelling and returning back to Edinburgh

If you are currently on international exchange at a non-European destination or participating in a short-term international experience, and you have specific questions about your situation, please contact: international.outgoing@ed.ac.uk

If you are on Erasmus+ exchange or a student participating in a European study or work away experience, please contact: europe.outgoing@ed.ac.uk

Students who are not on study exchange or work placement can discuss the issue with their School supervisor or Personal Tutor.

If you are a member of staff, please contact your line manager.

University travel to non-affected areas, including future travel plans

The University has now banned all non essential international travel with immediate effect from 13 March 2020 for a period of 2 months.

Even for essential travel, we would encourage all areas to consider contingency arrangements should the advice from the Foreign and Commonwealth Office (FCO) and University change. The Covid-19 outbreak is a rapidly developing situation and it is difficult to predict how things may change in the UK or internationally over the coming weeks and months and it is difficult to anticipate what advice will be issued in the future, and the impact this will have on travellers. 

Consideration should be given to whether the travel is essential, whether it can be accomplished in another manner (for example Skype or in a different location) or postponed till a date in the future or indefinitely. What are the arrangements should individuals not be allowed to return from an area.  This should be taken into consideration when undertaking a Business Travel Risk Assessment.

Travel risk assessment

We encourage all staff and students to undertake a travel risk assessment prior to any travel on University business. The Business Travel website contains a vast array of guidance and advice in relation to travel.

Travel health clinics

We remind staff that the Occupational Health Service provide travel health clinics, please see the Travel Health pages for more information. However, there is currently no advice regarding any vaccinations or other precautions, bar the advice highlighted by Public Health England and Health Protection Scotland.

University Travel - Key Travel Bookings

University related travel for business or study should be booked using the University’s travel provider, Key Travel (secured).

Due to the ongoing Coronavirus outbreak, Key Travel has seen a sharp increase in the number of calls and emails they are receiving.  All enquiries are being dealt with as quickly as possible, however to assist in dealing with enquiries, Key Travel has requested that travellers follow the advice below:

For existing bookings
  • If you booked via email or phone - contact Key Travel via email for cancellations.
  • If you booked online and are trying to cancel, please make your cancellation online.

Please do not send another email or call about a request already made as it adds to the large number of enquiries.

 

For all new travel enquiries:
  • Please book online using their online tool KT Online (secured).
  • For travel within the next week – please call Key Travel directly
  • For non-urgent travel – please book online or send Key Travel an email
