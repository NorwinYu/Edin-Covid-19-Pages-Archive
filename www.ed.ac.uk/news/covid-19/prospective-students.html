Prospective students

Information for prospective students who have applied to study at the University of Edinburgh.

Edinburgh is a truly global university with an ongoing commitment to international diversity and a community in which students and staff feel valued and welcome.

The current outbreak of coronavirus is likely to have some impact on you wherever you live across the world, although we realise that the impact is more acute in countries where it has become established. We understand you may be concerned about how this could affect your application, and we want to keep you informed and supported throughout this uncertain period.

We have provided answers to key questions below, and as the situation develops, we will update these with the latest information we have.

 

University of Edinburgh on-campus events

Here at the University of Edinburgh, our priority is the health and wellbeing of our students, staff, and visitors.

Recognising the rapidly evolving impact of Covid-19, we have taken the difficult decision to cancel on-campus offer holder events. We are working to develop an online alternative for offer holders and we will be in touch with you soon with more information on this.

We have also decided to cancel the Undergraduate Open Day scheduled for 15 June this year.

The two undergraduate Open Days scheduled for 26 September and 10 October 2020 are currently still planned to go ahead, and this webpage will be updated with the latest guidance. We are also working to develop an online alternative for prospective students thinking about starting University in 2021.

Our offer making timeline

We value all of our applicants, and we are committed to making decisions on their applications in a timely and evidence-based way.

However, with continuing disruption to education services in many parts of the world, we are having to review how best to support applicants at this time.    

For this reason, we expect to extend our offer-making period while we wait to see what mitigations will be put in place by universities and awarding bodies in affected countries for students who have not yet completed their qualifications. If your application is based on predicted grades and you are impacted by school or university closures, you may find that the admissions decision is delayed. 

We will be closely monitoring the situation and will take appropriate action as mitigations put in place by institutions and awarding bodies become clear. We will update the information on this page as soon as possible. Please note that individual admissions teams will not be able to provide any further information because these decisions are being taken centrally.

If all schools or universities in your country are closed

We are aware that a number of areas are facing widespread school and university closures and affected students will be anxious about the impact this may have on their application. We strongly encourage you to take advantage of any opportunity of any online provision from your institution or exam board to ensure you are as well prepared as possible to take your exams when the situation is resolved.

We are receiving updates from schools, agents, partner institutions, the British Council, and awarding bodies, and will take any steps we can to minimise the impact for you.

If your local school is closed or may close

In some countries individual schools or universities may have closed or teaching may be restricted due to local circumstances. We expect any relevant adjustments to be made by exam boards or awarding bodies but we will monitor the situation. If you are affected, we strongly encourage you to take advantage of any opportunity of any online provision available from your institution or exam board to ensure you are as well prepared as possible when your school or university reopens.

If you cannot take exams

If you are waiting to take school or university qualifications in a country where the education system is closed, you may not know yet if you will be able to take your exams as planned.

Many exam boards and awarding bodies have published advice and information about the steps they are taking to help those affected. Where assessments take place in school, there may be special arrangements for students to attend. We would advise you to contact your school or test centre, or the exam board websites, to find out what they have put in place to help.

  • Pearson
  • College Board

We will continue to monitor the information provided by awarding bodies.

If the English language test centres are shut

We know that test providers are intending to offer additional test appointments once they reopen to deal with the backlog.

We will continue to monitor the provision of English language tests over the coming weeks, and will assess the impact this is having on our offer holders, to identify if there are any actions we can take to alleviate problems with access to tests.

Further information will be made available on this page as soon as it is available.

If you can’t obtain documents

If your school or university is closed, you may not be able to provide all of the documents we have asked for, such as transcripts or references, at this time. If this is a problem for you, please contact your admissions team who will be able to review your deadlines.

Contact information is provided on the relevant page of the online degree finder.

If the visa centres are closed

We are aware that visa centres have been closed for several weeks now in some countries. Students who had been due to start a degree in March, April or May and who have been unable to apply for their visa should contact the Student Immigration Service to discuss their options.

We hope that visa centres will reopen before other students will need to apply for visas, but will keep the situation under review.

 

Pre-sessional English for Academic Purposes

The University of Edinburgh is aware that delays to processing visas and/or obtaining English Language qualifications across the world may have an impact on applications for the Pre-sessional English for Academic Purposes. 

We are committed to providing the 7- and 10-week Pre-sessional English for Academic Purposes programme and can confirm that we will be offering the programme online this year. The programme, course content and learning outcomes will remain exactly as advertised on our website, but the delivery of the programme will change from in-person to online. 

If you have a confirmed place on the programme, you do not need to re-apply. Your place remains confirmed for the online pre-sessional programme and English Language Education will be in contact regarding further details. If you have not yet applied for the programme, you wish to apply now, please visit the English Language Education website.

Visiting and Exchange students

We would encourage all potential Visiting Students to apply as they would normally. We are putting into place longer deadlines for the submission of university transcripts and English language certificates as well as working on ways to be flexible should we need to be, when deadlines become nearer.

Applying for accomodation 

Accommodation applications are open as normal.  Accommodation offers aren’t made until you have been given an unconditional offer for your University course, and accommodation contracts are not sent before the beginning of May.  For more information on the University’s accommodation guarantee and properties, please visit the student accomodation webpage.
