Prospective students

Information for prospective students who have applied to study at the University of Edinburgh.

The University will be open and ready to teach at the start of the new academic year.

We know it is critical that your education is all that you expected it to be and that it is provided in a safe and welcoming environment. We are delighted you want to come to Edinburgh and we want to give you everything you wanted from us, despite the challenges.

We intend to be ready to teach despite any travel restrictions, wherever our students are in the world. As we adapt to this Covid-19 pandemic we are shaping our delivery to ensure that all students - whether here in Edinburgh with us, or elsewhere in the world and joining us a little later in the academic year when circumstances allow - will be fully part of our community.

This may mean we change the format or order of our teaching, but be reassured that our Edinburgh graduate qualities will be as excellent as ever. Changes will vary by degree and individual Schools will contact offer-holders over the coming weeks to confirm how these changes will apply.

How do I pay for my accommodation? (Updated 11 June)

The University of Edinburgh offer flexible payments plans so you can choose the option that suits your best. You can either pay in full or in instalments.  For more information on payment options visit: https://www.accom.ed.ac.uk/for-students/paying-your-rent/

Will I be able to quarantine on arrival at University accommodation and what will this mean? 

We anticipate being able to support International students who will be staying in University accommodation if there is a requirement of 14 days quarantine on entry into the UK.  We are currently looking at how we can facilitate this and will update these FAQs as soon as more information is available.

What health and safety measures are you putting in place for accommodation in September? (Updated 11 June) 

We want to assure you that your health and wellbeing is our top priority. We currently have a large number of students living in our accommodation during Covid-19 lockdown restrictions and have procedures in place in our residences to comply with government lockdown measures. Our accommodation, catering and events team are working hard over the summer on additional health and safety procedures ready for the start of term. We will be in touch with all students who book accommodation at the University of Edinburgh to confirm more details shortly.

Is there support during any period of self-isolation if I'm not staying in University accommodation? (Updated 11 June)

Our health and wellbeing services, including the Chaplaincy, Student Counselling and Student Disability Services will support your physical and mental wellbeing, from near and afar, individually and collectively.

Health and Wellbeing

If I can't arrive at the start of term due to travel restrictions, what does this mean for my accommodation lease? 

If you can’t travel to Edinburgh in September, but are still a matriculated student and expect to come to campus at some stage in the future, we will either (depending on the availability of bedrooms):

  • hold your room for you until you can arrive; or
  • release you from your lease asking you to re-contact us when you know you can you travel and we will do our best to find new accommodation for you at this point.

If my circumstances change and I no longer plan on coming to the University of Edinburgh in September, will I be held to my lease?

You will not be held to your accommodation lease if your circumstances change and you are unable to come to the University of Edinburgh as a result of the Covid-19 pandemic.

What support is available for University accommodation? (Updated 11 June)

The University of Edinburgh has a Residence Life team whose aim is to create a residential community that welcomes and supports all students living in our accommodation. The team are here to help with your transition into life in Edinburgh, to meet others and to make the most of your time at University.  For more information on Residence Life and what they do visit: https://www.accom.ed.ac.uk/for-students/residence-life/

Do you get put in touch with your 'flatmates' before arrival in September? (Updated 11 June)

The University of Edinburgh’s Residence Life team run Facebook pages for each accommodation block and before you arrive in September you will be invited to join giving you the chance to virtually meet some of your new neighbours and flatmates and to ask any questions you may have.

Private sector accommodation

Cancelling a lease with a private landlord or letting agent

If you have entered into a lease (also known as a Private Rented Tenancy) after December 2017, you can terminate your agreement at any point by providing your landlord at least 28 days' notice in writing. 

Advice on private housing issues is available from The Advice Place and more information on Private Rented Tenancies can be found at  https://www.gov.scot/publications/private-residential-tenancies-tenants-guide/

What will happen to my deposit if I give notice on my lease?

In Scotland, all deposits paid in relation to accommodation are covered by the Tenancy Deposit Schemes (Scotland) Regulations 2011.  Landlords and letting agents who receive deposits in Scotland are required to register with their local authority and join a tenancy deposit scheme.  Your deposit acts as a guarantee against damage to the property, cleaning bills if the property is left in poor condition, unpaid bills, including rent. If there are no such issues at the end of your tenancy, you should expect your deposit to be returned to you.  

The Advice Place can provide advice on this issue and more information on the Tenancy Deposit Scheme can be found at https://www.mygov.scot/tenancy-deposits-tenants/

 Applications

If you want to defer your offer (Updated 11 June)

Our approach to deferrals has always recognised that individual offer-holders may face significant personal challenges or circumstances which mean that they are unable to start their studies as originally planned. While we recognise that all students are likely to have faced some level of disruption from Covid-19, we also acknowledge that some applicants will find their ability to take up their studies particularly affected. This includes:

  • Students acting as essential workers who may be unable to relinquish their duties before the start of the semester
  • Students affected by personal illness, injury or mental health problems
  • Students who have experienced family illness or bereavement
  • Students with caring responsibilities or dependents
  • Students facing financial hardship or disruption of funding
  • Athletes in preparation for rescheduled major sporting events

We will prioritise deferral requests from these students.

While recognising the increased levels of general uncertainty associated with the Covid-19 pandemic, we are also aware of the risks to academic momentum associated with deferral and of the need to treat applicants in different intake years on a fair basis.  

Consequently, while deferrals will be available, availability will be limited.   This will be particularly true for high demand programmes, to avoid imposing a material disadvantage on 2021 applicants by a reduction in the range of places available to them. 

Undergraduate deferral requests

Deferral requests for undergraduate degrees which are received by 5.00pm on 30 June 2020 will receive a decision by 6 July 2020. If a deferral request is unsuccessful, the offer for 2020 entry will remain valid.

We expect to make most of our deferral decisions by 6 July, but we may be able to consider a limited number of additional requests received over the summer and intend to make these decisions after 20th August. 

If we receive more requests for deferred places than we are able to approve, we will consider any specific information on personal extenuating circumstances provided by students on an individual basis. Otherwise, we will prioritise those who are best qualified for their programme, taking account of any contextual considerations.

Unfortunately due to UCAS regulations we are only able to defer a place where all offer conditions have been met by 31 August 2020. If your results are expected after this date then we will honour your offer for 2020 entry but we will not be able to defer the place.

Postgraduate taught deferral requests

Decisions on deferral requests will begin to be made from late June 2020.

It is expected that some deferral decisions for each programme will be made each month until the 31 August.  Unsuccessful deferral requests will be rolled forward to the next decision point.

If we receive more requests for deferred places than we are able to approve, we will consider any specific mitigating information provided by students on an individual basis. Otherwise, we will prioritise those students who are best qualified for their programme, taking account of any contextual considerations.

Where a deposit is required, a student must have paid their deposit in order to have their offer deferred. The deposit will roll forward with the deferred offer.

If you are waiting for a decision on your application

The Covid-19 pandemic is having a huge impact on the way we live and work worldwide. Here in Edinburgh, our admissions staff are observing the UK-wide lockdown and working remotely. We realise that you may be concerned about any impact this will have on your application to study with us, and we would like to give you some reassurance. 

Our admissions staff have access to all our systems and are continuing to process applications as normal. Any academic staff involved in making admissions decisions are also able to participate remotely.

However, this new way of working will take a little time to settle down and it does mean you may see a delay in decision making on your application. Please bear with us as we make these adjustments. We will make decisions as quickly as we can, and we will communicate these with you as normal.

If your school or college is closed

At the moment, schools, colleges and universities across the world are facing closures– and we understand that this is causing great concern for students planning to begin their degrees this year. You may also be worried about how you will meet the conditions of your offer. We have further information about the cancellation of exams and assessments on this page.

Meanwhile, we encourage you to take advantage of any relevant online study opportunities available to you, to ensure you are fully prepared for your future studies.

If your university is closed

We are aware that many universities are closed and you may be worried about whether you will be able to complete your current degree in time to meet the conditions of your offer. We know many universities have moved teaching online and are arranging alternative assessments. 

We will send you updated information on the evidence we need of your final grades, and the deadline for providing this, as soon as possible. We will be as flexible as possible, while making sure you have time to get your visa if you require one.

English language requirements

Updates to accepted English language tests (Updated 12 June)

All students

Updated 22 May: LanguageCert International ESOL

We have approved the LanguageCert International ESOL online English test for entry to the University of Edinburgh in 2020.

This is an online English language qualification that you can take at home no matter where you live.

There are two tests:

  • Reading/Writing/Listening
  • Speaking

These can be taken at the same time, or separately. You must take both tests to meet our entry requirements. 

You will need to send us your LanguageCert International ESOL e-certificate and your full scores, so that we can verify your grades.

For more information, and to find out the levels you will require to meet our entry requirements, please see our website:

LanguageCert International ESOL for undergraduate entry 

LanguageCert International ESOL for postgraduate entry 

TOEFL iBT Home Edition

We have approved the TOEFL iBT Special Home Edition online test for entry to the University of Edinburgh. This test is available in many countries around the world, and can be taken online from your home.

Entry requirements based on the TOEFL iBT Special Home Edition test are exactly the same as the entry requirements for the standard TOEFL iBT test which are listed in your offer.

For more information and to book a test, please go online: TOEFL iBT Special Home Edition 

Postgraduate only 

Updated 22 May: additions to our list of approved universities

We are continuing to assess universities in non-majority English speaking countries that teach and assess in English to determine whether degrees from these universities can be used to meet our English language requirements.

We have recently added a number of institutions in Botswana, Ghana, Kenya, Namibia, Nigeria, Rwanda, South Africa, Tanzania, Zambia and Zimbabwe to our list of approved universities.

If your previous degree was awarded from one of our approved universities within 3.5 years of the start date for your chosen degree at Edinburgh, this can be used to meet our English language entry requirements.

Students from mainland China and Iran

Is the Duolingo English Test accepted?

We will only accept the Duolingo English Test (DET) for entry to a degree programme where no other accepted English language test is available to you.

Currently this is likely to apply to students from China and Iran only.

We cannot accept Duolingo English Test for entry to a postgraduate degree if you already hold an unconditional offer for our Pre-Sessional English programme or for an alternative BALEAP-accredited UK pre-sessional English language course.

For more information, please visit our Duolingo pages for undergraduate or postgraduate entry.

If the English language test centres are closed  

We are also aware that English language test centres are closed in many countries. You may be concerned about being unable to take your English language test, or unable to use your school qualifications, to meet the English language conditions of your offer.

It is important for the success of your studies in Edinburgh that you can demonstrate good English language skills. If you come from outside the EU and you need a visa to study here, the UK government may also set an English language requirement as a condition of your visa. While we hope test centres will reopen soon in your country, we will continue to review other options and will keep you informed if we make any changes to accepted English language tests. 

Please see our Updates to accepted English language tests section on this page for further information.

Pre-sessional English for Academic Purposes

The University of Edinburgh Pre-Sessional English for Academic Purposes programme is available to students who have a conditional offer for a postgraduate degree.

We are aware that delays to processing visas and/or obtaining English language qualifications across the world may have an impact on applications for the Pre-sessional English for Academic Purposes. 

We are committed to providing the 7- and 10-week Pre-sessional English for Academic Purposes programme and can confirm that we will be offering the programme online this year. The programme, course content and learning outcomes will remain exactly as advertised on our website, but the delivery of the programme will change from in-person to online. 

If you have a confirmed place on the programme, you do not need to re-apply. Your place remains confirmed for the online pre-sessional programme and English Language Education will be in contact regarding further details. If you have not yet applied for the programme, you wish to apply now, please visit the English Language Education website.
