Study and work placements

Information for current students on work placements and field trips, including study/work placements abroad.

Please note that, in light of the lockdown restrictions implemented by the UK and Scottish Governments on 23 March 2020, only ‘essential-services’ should be operating on campus. We will be updating these FAQs regularly with the latest guidance.

 Work placements and field trips

I am a taught student. How will the University replace my lab-work, field trip or exhibition?

  • Schools are making arrangements to ensure that you can be assessed based on whatever experiments, studio projects etc. you have completed prior to the move to remote teaching. Where laboratory- or studio-based projects have not yet begun (e.g. for PGT students) Schools will put in place alternative, desk-based projects in order to assess students.
  • Where field trips have been cancelled, Schools will be amending learning outcomes for those courses (if needed) in order to ensure that students are not disadvantaged.
  • We are working on alternative approaches to assessing students for practice-based courses and programmes, including finding ways to allow students on these courses such as Art, Architecture etc. to showcase their work.

I was due to undertake a field trip or placement in the summer but this has been cancelled. Will the trip be moved back to a later date?

Most field trip and placement activity that has been cancelled will be assessed in an alternative way this academic year, via a method that requires desk-based research only – you will not be required to be on-site to undertake the learning/ assessment.

The only exceptions to this will be in situations where your programme is accredited by a Professional Statutory or Regulatory Body (PSRB) and the learning outcomes cannot be replaced because they are required for PSRB accreditation – for example, clinical hours. Where this is the case, your School will keep you up to date as to the arrangements for allowing you to obtain the necessary experience and learning outcomes.

Can I undertake a placement or field trip activity if it is based near my home/ permanent place of residence?

If your placement or field trip is due to take place before 31 July 2020, then no – unfortunately, you cannot participate in it. We have taken this decision based on the latest guidance available regarding the Covid-19 pandemic, with the health and safety of our students first and foremost in mind.

If your placement or field trip is due to take place after 1st August 2020, then your School will be actively reviewing it and a decision will be taken nearer to this date to assess whether it is viable, on the basis of any updated Government information available at the time. You should not book any travel or accommodation for your trip without the prior approval of your School. Please note that your School may have to take a decision to cancel trips after 1st August as this situation develops, and you will be advised as soon as possible if this is the case.

I am on a programme with a clinical placement – what will happen in relation to my placement?

This depends on your professional body or clinical provider. Your School or Deanery will issue information directly about your clinical placement.

Students on exchange/traineeship abroad in 2019/20

Are study exchanges and overseas work placements scheduled to begin before 31st July still going ahead? (NEW) 

Given the uncertainty of the Covid-19 pandemic and the resulting impact on the study and work away environment, to protect the safety of our students, their families and our partners, the University has decided to cancel all study exchanges and overseas work placements scheduled to begin before 31st July 2020. If this affects you directly, please contact the Study and Work Away Service for further guidance.

This decision also includes all overseas group travel, field trips, summer school or placements that were due to take place before 31st July. However, this excludes programmes in CMVM and the School of Health and Social Sciences, which will continue due to prioritised medical and clinical requirements.

We are continually monitoring changes in travel restrictions and immigration policies and will review this guidance as required for exchanges and placements scheduled to take place after 31st July.

Should I return home from my placement? 

The University of Edinburgh is now formally advising all students currently on study/work placements abroad to return to their permanent residence if they are able to do so, and if they have a safe place to return to.  This is based on the exceptional travel advisory notices issued by the Foreign & Commonwealth Office (FCO) on 23 March 2020, urging all British citizens currently abroad to return to the UK now, if commercial flights are still available. We are aware that similar guidance has been issued to citizens of other countries.

However, we understand that not all students are in a situation where it would be safe to travel, due to restrictions, concerns about exposing themselves to risks by travelling through busy airports, family circumstances etc. For those who feel that it is safer to remain in your host country, we will continue to support you as best we can in-country for the duration of your period abroad. You will need to make sure that you have a safe and comfortable place to stay and be mindful that you may need to stay longer than initially planned, if travel restrictions or lack of travel options prevent you from returning home at the end of your exchange. Please also make sure you keep monitoring the FCO travel advice for your host country, and continue to follow the relevant health and safety advice.

Who should I contact if I need to make changes to my placement? 

Please make sure you let the Study and Work Away team (SWAY) know if you intend to make any changes to your placement, for example, returning to your home country to complete your placement remotely. You will have received emails from SWAY with a link to the relevant survey asking you to provide us with information about your circumstances. If you have any other questions relating to your placement, please contact Europe.outgoing@ed.ac.uk (for European placements) or international.outgoing@ed.ac.uk (for international placements).

Will the University’s travel insurance cover any additional costs of me returning home (e.g. flights, accommodation, etc.)? 

Please refer to the insurance FAQs for details about what the University of Edinburgh’s travel insurance would cover.

If you are experiencing financial hardship and would be unable to cover the initial cost of returning home, please contact studentfunding@ed.ac.uk, who will be able to advise on the support available.

Can I continue with my placement abroad?

As outlined above, if you are unable to travel to your normal place of residence and/or feel that it is safer to remain in your host country than to travel, we will continue to support you as best we can in-country for the duration of your period abroad.

You will need to make sure that you have a safe and comfortable place to stay and be mindful that you may need to stay longer than initially planned, if travel restrictions or lack of travel options prevent you from returning home at the end of your exchange. Please also make sure you continue to follow the relevant health and safety advice, and contact the Study and Work Away service (SWAY) if you require further support.

Can I continue with my placement remotely?

Many students will be able to complete their academic requirements remotely (e.g. through virtual classes/remote exams or remote working options provided by your host organisation). You should check the remote options offered by your host, and if they have arrangements in place for you to complete your placement remotely then we would encourage you to make use of them, to ensure you achieve the learning outcomes set out in your learning/training agreement, and the associated credits.

If I return home from my placement, will I still be able to receive credits and progress?

As outlined above, many students who return home will be able to complete their academic requirements remotely (e.g. through virtual classes/remote exams or remote working options provided by your host). If this is the case, you should continue with your placement remotely, which should enable you to gain credits and progress as normal. If continuing remotely isn’t an option for you, please see the section below.

My host institution does not provide any online resources to enable me to continue with my placement remotely. Will the UoE put something in place for me?

Yes. If you are unable to continue with your placement remotely because your host institution is not offering online resources to enable you to complete your assessments remotely, then the University of Edinburgh will put in place alternative arrangements for you so that you will not be academically disadvantaged by returning home.

My host institution is closed but it is not safe/possible for me to leave my host country. What are my options?

A large number of partner institutions have now temporarily closed/suspended teaching and are instead offering remote courses/assessments/working options. Please make sure you keep up to date with the services provided by your host, and are able to access any online resources provided, whilst you are unable to attend in-person.

As mentioned above, if you are unable to leave your host country we will continue to support you as best we can in-country for the duration of your period abroad.

If you need to remain in your host country but your host institution is not offering online resources to enable you to complete your assessments off-campus whilst it is closed, then the University of Edinburgh will put in place alternative arrangements for you so that you will not be academically disadvantaged by returning home.

What if I can’t return to my home country due to travel restrictions?

If you are able to leave your host country but cannot enter your normal country of residence due to travel restrictions, please contact Accom.Reslife@ed.ac.uk to arrange emergency accommodation in Edinburgh.

If you are unable to leave your host country due to travel restrictions, then our current focus would be on supporting you to remain safely in-country until you are able to book a flight out. If you are in this situation, you should keep monitoring the FCO travel advice, follow any advice from the local authorities, and contact the SWAY team on international.outgoing@ed.ac.uk or Europe.outgoing@ed.ac.uk (depending on your destination) if you require further support.

If I return home and complete my placement remotely, will I still be eligible for my Erasmus+ grant?

Yes. All students who are able to complete their mobility, either remotely or in-person, will be entitled to receive their full Erasmus+ grant, regardless of whether you have returned home or are staying in your host country.

This means that if you have returned home and are continuing your exchange remotely through the online material provided by your host university, you will be eligible for your full Erasmus+ grant. The SWAY team will be in touch with students in this situation to let you know what documentation will be required to enable us to pay out second instalment grant payments (that are normally processed following completion of the mobility).

What are the implications on my Erasmus+ grant if I return home but cannot continue my placement remotely?

If you have returned home and are not able to continue with your studies remotely then, as per Erasmus+ regulations, you will only be able to retain funding covering the actual period of time spent abroad. This amount could be lower than the amount you received in your first instalment, meaning that you may have to return part of your funding. However, if you have incurred additional costs strictly related to your mobility (such as e.g. travel and/or in-country accommodation expenses) you may be eligible for extra funding, up to a maximum of your total original grant amount. Please make sure you keep any relevant receipts. The SWAY team will contact you to let you know what the next steps are you need to take.

If I remain in my host country and continue my placement remotely, will I still be eligible for my Erasmus+ grant?

If you have remained in your host country whilst studying/working remotely, you will be eligible to receive your Erasmus+ grant as you would under normal circumstances. 

Students due to go on exchange / traineeship abroad in 2020/21

Will my placement in 2020/21 still go ahead? (NEW)

The Foreign and Commonwealth Office (FCO ) is currently advising against all but essential international travel, and the University of Edinburgh has subsequently restricted all staff and student international travel to “essential travel only”. Based on this, the University has taken the decision to cancel all international study/work placements that are due to begin before 31 July 2020. This means that if you are due to undertake a study/work placement abroad before 31 July 2020, you will not be able to proceed with your placement for semester 1.

If your placement is due to take place after 31 July 2020, then our advice at present is that it will go ahead for the time being. However, due to the fast changing nature of the global Covid-19 pandemic, the University is monitoring the situation very closely, and we will be in touch as soon as possible if this advice changes.

My host university has an upcoming application deadline. Should I submit my application? (NEW)

We will continue to nominate students according to partner university procedures and deadlines, and encourage you to follow the application instructions you receive from us/your host institution as normal.

If you are unable to proceed with your exchange in semester 1, we would still advise you to complete your host university’s application according to their deadline. This may help facilitate you postponing your exchange to semester 2 if this is possible. We will be consulting with your School and our partner institutions regarding alternative options, and will advise on this in due course.

Can I book travel/ accommodation for my placement now? (NEW)

We would always advise students to refrain from paying for travel or accommodation for an international placement until you have received written acceptance from your host organisation, and you have received your visa (if applicable). In addition to this, given the current situation, we would advise students going on placement in the autumn of 2020 to hold off making travel or accommodation arrangements for the time being.

If your placement is due to start in August 2020, we would advise that you contact the Study and Work Away (SWAY) team in advance of making any travel and/or accommodation arrangements. You can find our contact details below.

My host university has an upcoming deadline to apply for student accommodation and/or requests a deposit. What should I do?(NEW)

We recognise that students going on study exchange may need to apply for student accommodation over the coming months, when there is still a degree of uncertainty surrounding upcoming travel. In this case, we’d advise you to apply for the accommodation as normal so as not to miss out on a room, and if your placement is cancelled any amounts paid will be covered by either the University travel insurance or by the University directly. Please inform the SWAY team before making any financial commitments related to your upcoming placement – see contact details below.

I have already booked travel and/or accommodation for my placement. Will the University cover these costs if my placement is subsequently cancelled? (NEW) 

If you have already incurred travel and/or accommodation costs for your placement and it is subsequently cancelled, you should contact your travel and accommodation provider in the first instance to request a refund.  If you are unable to claim a reimbursement through your travel and accommodation provider, you may be able to claim under the University travel insurance or from the University directly

I have been informed by my host institution that my placement has been cancelled or delayed. What are my options? (NEW)

We are aware that a number of partner institutions have taken the decision to suspend incoming exchanges/placements for semester 1 2020/21. We will let you know if we become aware that your host is no longer accepting incoming students in semester 1 2020/21. The University is currently looking into possible alternative arrangements for students who are not able to go abroad in semester 1, and we will provide further information on this in due course.

I have a compulsary international placement as part of my degree programme. How will I meet my academic requirements if my placement cannot go ahead? (NEW)

If you have a compulsory placement as part of your degree, the University will put in place alternative arrangements to allow you to meet the requirements of your degree programme. The details of these arrangements will vary from programme to programme, and will be communicated to you directly by your School in the event that your placement is cancelled.

Who should I contact if I still have questions about my 2020/21 placement? (NEW)

  • Students due to go on exchange in Europe should contact Europe.outgoing@ed.ac.uk
  • Students due to go on exchange outside of Europe should contact international.outgoing@ed.ac.uk
  • Students due to go on an Erasmus+ Traineeship should contact workabroad@ed.ac.uk

Please note that we are currently receiving a high volume of emails and will be prioritising emails from students in need of urgent assistance. We thank you in advance for your understanding and patience.
