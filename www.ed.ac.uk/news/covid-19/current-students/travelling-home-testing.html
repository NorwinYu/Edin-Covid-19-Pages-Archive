Travelling home and asymptomatic testing

Information on voluntary asymptomatic testing and how you can get your tests.

We know that many of you will be making plans for your winter holidays. We understand that some of you will be remaining in Edinburgh while others will be going home to see family. With many thousands of students moving around the country before the holidays, it’s really important that we all do what we can to reduce the risk of community transmission of Covid-19 as much as possible.

On 11th November, the Scottish Government announced plans to provide Covid-19 testing on campuses for all students who wish to travel home in the UK for the winter break. You will be able to get a test even if you are showing no symptoms. If you test negative, you will know that it is safe to travel home.

Read the Scottish Government’s statement

We have been putting arrangements for on-campus testing in place. Further information is available below, and we will update this as soon as arrangements have been finalised.

How testing will work

The Scottish Government is working with institutions and the UK Government’s testing programme to offer lateral flow testing – a new rapid test which can deliver a result within 30 minutes.

Each student will be offered two lateral flow tests between 3 and 5 days apart. These tests will be administered on campus, by the University. The tests use a self-administered swab from the nose and throat, done under supervision at one of the University’s testing locations. If you test negative you will know that you can travel home with minimal risk of infecting others.

If either tests are positive, you will need to self-isolate. However, due to the timing of the tests in early-December and after the period of self-isolation, you should still be able to return home in time for Christmas.

Who should get tested – and why

The scheme is voluntary, but we are urging any student who wishes to travel home in the UK for the winter break to get tested.

While around half of people with Covid-19 develop symptoms, many show no symptoms (asymptomatic) and are unaware that they are infected. Even if you are asymptomatic, you can still spread the virus. The aim of these tests is to find these asymptomatic cases and minimise the risk of spreading the virus further when you travel home for the winter break.

So these tests will be of interest to all students who are due to travel home in the UK, but particularly those who may have vulnerable family members at home, and those who are due to travel home on public transport

Testing centre locations and opening times

Testing centres will be open from Monday 30 November to Wednesday 9 December inclusive, from 10:00 to 20:30 daily.

Testing centres will be located at:

  • Pleasance Sport Complex and Gym, 46 Pleasance, Edinburgh EH8 9TJ
  • Main Games Hall, St Leonard's Land, Holyrood Road, Edinburgh EH8 8AQ

We will share further details about how to book a test with you soon.

Safety measures at testing sites

Testing sites will operate physical distancing along with queue management. Staff will wear appropriate PPE and visitors will be asked to wear face coverings except when they are carrying out a test. Testing areas will be cleaned between each use. 

How to book a test

All tests will need to be booked in advance and we will update this information as soon as possible with further details.

Getting results

The results will be sent to you within 24 hours of each test.

Receiving a positive test

In line with Scottish Government guidelines, if either tests are positive, you and your close contacts will need to self-isolate. These test results will also trigger NHS Test and Protect, and you will need to attend a NHS testing facility for a further PCR (polymerase chain reaction) test for confirmation.

However, due to the timing of the tests in early December, you should still be able to return home in time for the winter break once your period of self-isolation is complete.

Supporting students in self-isolation

If you need to self-isolate, you will need to let the University know by completing our self-isolation contact form. We can then ensure you receive all available support to help you during this period.

You must also inform us again, using the form, if you receive a positive test result for Covid-19. This must be done even if you have already completed the form to advise us that you are self-isolating.

International and domestic travel

International flights for those students travelling home for winter can still be taken, as 'travel for students returning home from university or college at the end of term' is an exception to travel restrictions currently in place. This also applies to travel within the UK and across 'Levels'.

Covid-19 test required for entry to some countries

Certain countries require a negative Covid-19 test to allow travel to that country. It is your responsibility to check entry requirements for any travel you plan to undertake.

The asymptomatic testing offered by the University testing centres is not sufficient evidence for a ‘fit to fly’ note. Pre-travel tests are not available on the NHS and must be purchased privately from companies who offer that service. In Edinburgh, these include the following, although there may be others and none are endorsed by the University of Edinburgh:

  • NHS Lothian Travel Clinic
  • TrExMed Travel Clinic
  • Edinburgh Travel Clinic
  • Newington Pharmacy
  • CityDoc
  • Vivo Clinic

Countries may specify a certain type of test, ensure you confirm your chosen provider of the test can comply with this prior to paying for any testing.

Updating your location

We will contact you shortly with a brief survey so that you can let us know if you expect to be on campus over the winter break, and if not, when you expect to travel. This will help us map out the likely pattern of departures and ensure support is in place for students staying over the winter break. 

Students on placements

The testing system has been set up to test the large number of students who will travel home before, or very soon after, 9th December. If you will be on a placement after this then there is no requirement for you to undertake this testing. Reducing social mixing before travelling

Any student who wants to return home should voluntarily reduce their social mixing for two weeks before going home. This means going out only for essential reasons and exercise.

Will the rest of the Semester be online?

We are currently providing teaching and learning as part of a hybrid approach, which is a combination of on-campus and online experiences. There are no current plans to change this approach, but we will let you know immediately if the position changes.

Travelling back to Edinburgh after the winter break

We are awaiting a further announcement from the Scottish Government on plans for students returning to campus in January, and will update you as soon as possible.

Data protection

The minimum possible personal data will be used by the University of Edinburgh to help facilitate the screening programme. Most of this data – such as your name, email address and accommodation address – will already be held by the University.
