Information about Semester 2

The latest information and guidance about our Semester Two plans, returning to Edinburgh in January and accessing support.

To find out more about The University's support and guidance for Semester Two, visit our student webpages:

Information for students

Latest Scottish Government guidance 

On Monday 4th January the Scottish Government announced further restrictions that are being brought in across Scotland following the emergence of a new, highly contagious strain of the Coronavirus.

We have published further information below about what the new restrictions will mean for you and returning to University.

For the latest Scottish Government advice, please visit:

  • Stay at home guidance
  • Student Information Scotland

Travel restrictions (UPDATED: 6 January)

Travel between Scotland and the rest of the UK is not legal other than for specific exempt purposes (such as education). Students who have already travelled to their family homes elsewhere in the UK following our Asymptomatic Testing programme should remain there until the date of their planned return to term-time accommodation (please see our returning to campus FAQs below).

We continue to support those students who are currently staying in our University accommodation.

If you have yet to travel back to Edinburgh after the winter break, we are strongly encouraging you, wherever possible, not to return to your term time accommodation until such time as your on-campus teaching can resume as noted in our 'Phased return in January' FAQ below.

If, for essential purposes, you must return to campus, please follow Scottish travel guidance as well as travel guidance for other areas of the UK or any country you will be travelling from.​​​

Returning to campus

Phased return in January (UPDATED: 6 January)

We are reviewing the implications of the recent Government announcements for Semester 2 return. The information below is subject to review.

In December, we announced plans to welcome undergraduate students back to campus for Semester 2 in a series of phases. Your School will have been in touch with you at the time with full details.

Below we have outlined some key points about what the new restrictions will mean for you and your return to campus:

Undergraduates

  • All learning and teaching will restart on programmes of study during the week beginning 11th January 2021 digitally.
  • Students who were due to resume in-person, on-campus elements in the week commencing 4th and 11th January and had been contacted by their Schools can do so.
  • There is a delay to all those who were due to start the in-person, on-campus elements from 25th January. We do not know how long that delay will be but we are working with the Scottish Government to confirm details as soon as possible, as we realise how important this is for your planning.
  • Library and study facilities will remain open with existing health and safety measures in place.

Postgraduate taught programmes

  • All learning and teaching will restart on programmes of study during the week beginning 11th January 2021 digitally.
  • Students who were due to resume in-person, on-campus elements in the week commencing 11th January and had been contacted by their Schools can do so.
  • There is a delay to all those who were due to start the in-person, on-campus elements of their programme on or after 25th January 2021. Learning and teaching for this group of students will remain digital. We do not know how long that delay will be but we are working with the Scottish Government to confirm details as soon as possible, as we realise how important this is for your planning.

Postgraduate research

The new restrictions do not alter the information we are providing to postgraduate research students and essential research work may continue in our laboratories and other research facilities.

Please speak with your supervisor for further information on how these new restrictions may impact your work.

Postgraduate students  (UPDATED: 6 January)

Postgraduate taught programmes will restart as planned on 11 January digitally.

Students who were due to resume in-person, on-campus elements in the week commencing 11th January and had been contacted by their Schools can do so.

There is a delay to all those who were due to start the in-person, on-campus elements of their programme on or after 25th January 2021. Learning and teaching for this group of students will remain digital. We do not know how long that delay will be but we are working with the Scottish Government to confirm details as soon as possible, as we realise how important this is for your planning.

Please note this applies to on-campus postgraduate programmes only, with online postgraduate programmes continuing as planned.

The new guidance does not materially alter the information we are providing to postgraduate research students or research staff and essential research work may continue in our laboratories and other research facilities.

Please speak with your supervisor for further information on how these new restrictions may impact your work.

Please note that international postgraduate students who have not yet arrived in the UK should continue with their existing plans to arrive when they have secured their visa and made travel arrangements. Students who may need the dates on existing CAS or visas changed should contact studentimmigration@ed.ac.uk for help.

Travelling from another location in the UK back to Edinburgh (UPDATED: 6 January)

Travel for education is an exception to travel restrictions and applies to both domestic and international travel.

If you have yet to travel back to Edinburgh after the winter break, we are strongly encouraging you, wherever possible, not to return to your term time accommodation until such time as your on-campus teaching can resume as noted above.

If, for essential purposes, you must return to campus, please follow Scottish travel guidance as well as travel guidance for other areas of the UK or any country you will be travelling from.

Links to travel guidance for Scotland, the UK and international travel can be found below:

  • Scottish Government travel guidance
  • International travel and quarantine guidance
  • Country exemptions and information on the process for people entering the UK

You can return to University by private, family vehicle if you would prefer to avoid public transport. You should not share a car with anyone outside of your household, extended household or end of term household to minimise risks of transmission, unless there is no reasonable alternative.

If you choose to return using public transport, you should follow our guidance on how to travel safely. This includes wearing a face covering unless you are exempt, planning your journey in advance to avoid busy times and routes, washing/ sanitising your hands regularly, and keeping your distance while travelling where possible.

If you are travelling to Edinburgh from elsewhere in the UK, we strongly advise you to book an asymptomatic Covid-19 test as close to your return date as possible.

Book a test

Please be aware that the asymptomatic Covid-19 testing programme is not sufficient evidence to bypass self-isolation requirements if you are returning to Edinburgh from overseas.

Covid-19 testing

To support the safe return of our students to our campuses after the winter break, we will be offering asymptomatic Covid-19 testing from Thursday 7 January – Friday 12 February 2021.

The tests are voluntary and you do not need to have been tested in order to attend in-person teaching. But we strongly encourage you to book a test if you are returning to Edinburgh from another part of the UK. This will reduce the risk of you passing Covid-19 onto others when you and/or your housemates return to Edinburgh. 

Book a test

You should book a test as close to your return as possible - please remember that you must make two separate bookings using this form. If you are an undergraduate student, you should book your tests to coincide with the date you return to your term time accommodation. Your school should have been in contact with you regarding this.

Social interaction

All students are also being asked to voluntarily restrict their social interactions for 10 days before they return to University and for the same period once they arrive. 

When you arrive back at University, you should also minimise your social mixing for 10 days. This is to help minimise the transmission of the virus.

International travel to Edinburgh

International students will be able to return to Scotland as they have originally planned. You should check with your School in advance to confirm when your course will start and when you should arrive on campus.

If you are staying in University-managed accommodation, please let us know so that we can support you when you arrive.

Please note that the asymptomatic Covid-19 testing programme is not sufficient evidence to bypass self-isolation requirements if you are returning to Edinburgh from overseas.

You must follow the guidance on international travel and quarantine as appropriate when arriving back in Scotland.

Please read the Government’s International travel and quarantine guidance for the latest information on what you will need to do when entering the UK from another country:

  • International travel and quarantine guidance
  • Country exemptions and information on the process for people entering the UK

We are aware that some EU/international students will be unable to travel due to the new restrictions and we will of course support these students. If you are affected, please contact the Covid-19 hotline at +44 (0)131 370 0494.

Quarantine and self-isolation on your return to Edinburgh

If you need to quarantine on your return to Edinburgh (e.g. travelling back from a country not on the UK’s self-isolation exemption list), you will need to follow the usual self-isolation reporting process and inform the University once you are self-isolating in Edinburgh:

Inform the University if you are self-isolating

Support will continue for any student who needs to quarantine and for any students who may need to self-isolate.

Accommodation

Returning to your accommodation in Edinburgh (UPDATED: 6 January)

Our student accommodation remains open, and we will continue to support all students who are staying in our student accommodation.

If you have yet to travel back to Edinburgh after the winter break, we are strongly encouraging you, wherever possible, not to return to your term time accommodation until such time as your on-campus teaching can resume as noted in the FAQs above.

We recognise that some undergraduate students may need to return to campus earlier due to personal circumstances, such as employment purposes or health and wellbeing reasons. We will work to support those students on-campus.

Returning or new undergraduate students planning to travel to Scotland from overseas in January can arrive at any time but must follow the guidance on international travel including quarantining if they are from a non-exempt country. 

International students may return to accommodation in the new term as they originally planned and Schools will advise them about the wellbeing support available to them.

University accommodation

Students in university-managed accommodation will not be charged rent in the period from 11th January to their actual arrival date or the date of their phased return (whichever is earlier).

Who can apply for a rent pause?

  • If you are an Undergraduate student staying in University of Edinburgh accommodation, and
  • If you are leaving Edinburgh over the festive break and plan to return in line with Scottish Government / University guidance.

How to apply for a rent pause?

You can apply between the 18th December and 29th January 2021. Please follow the steps below to apply:

  1. Access your MyEd student portal via https://www.myed.ed.ac.uk
  2. Enter your username and password to login
  3. Click on the 'Student Life' tab and select 'Accommodation'
  4. Click 'Launch Services'
  5. Expand 'Term Time 2020 - 21'
  6. Click the ‘Special Request' button under the 'While You're Here' column
  7. Complete the information request.

Once you have completed your information you will receive a confirmation email with more detail on what will happen next.

When will you receive your rebate?

  • If you are paying by monthly instalments the rebate will be applied to your March accommodation payment
  • If you are paying by semester instalments the rebate will be applied to your April accommodation payment
  • If you have paid a full amount, you will receive a refund by end of April.

We do recognise many students did not leave Edinburgh during the winter break period. If you are a student who stayed in University of Edinburgh accommodation over the festive period or you are returning before 11 January 2021, you will not be eligible to receive this rent pause and your rent will be collected in the usual way.

Private accommodation

Students in privately managed University accommodation (such as Unite) or other private arrangements will need to contact their accommodation provider or landlord.

Learning and teaching

Important dates

Learning and teaching will begin in your programme of study in the week commencing 11th January 2021.

There is no change to the Semester end dates or Semester Two examination dates.

Hybrid learning (UPDATED: 6 January)

All learning and teaching will restart on programmes of study during the week beginning 11th January 2021 digitally.

We are working closely with the Scottish Government and we will increase our on-campus provisions once Covid-19 restrictions ease.

Support

Accessing support and using our facilities in Semester 2 (UPDATED: 5 January)

Our student support services will continue to operate – whether you are on-campus or not.

Due to the Scottish Government restrictions announced on 4 January, the Student Villages and University-managed cafes are currently closed. They will re-open subject to Government guidance.

Library and study space

Library and study facilities will remain open with existing health and safety measures in place.

Due to the latest Scottish Government restrictions, browsing access and book borrowing of the library collections is suspended. We are developing a new Scan & Deliver service which will provide some access to our print collections, and more information will be provided as this becomes available.&nbsp;

The Centre for Research Collections (CRC) will remain open.

Sport and exercise

The Pleasance Gym will be closed to members until at least Sunday 17 January 2021.

Sport & exercise

Semester 2 activities (UPDATED: 5 January)

We are reviewing what implications the recent Scottish Government announcement will have on in-person activities with our student societies. We will share more information about our plans with you soon.

Academic support

Your school will be in touch with you directly with further details about your course and Semester Two plans as soon as details have been confirmed.

If you have any questions about your course or programme, please contact your personal tutor or your School's student support team in the first instance.
