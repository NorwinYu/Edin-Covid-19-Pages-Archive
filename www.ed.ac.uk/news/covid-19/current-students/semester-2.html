Information about Semester 2

The latest information and guidance about our Semester Two plans, returning to Edinburgh in January and accessing support.

To find out more about The University's support and guidance for Semester Two, visit our student webpages:

Information for students

Latest Scottish Government guidance  (UPDATED: 15 January)

On Monday 4th January the Scottish Government announced further restrictions that are being brought in across Scotland following the emergence of a new, highly contagious strain of the Coronavirus. On Friday 8th January, the Scottish Government announced that university students will be taught online throughout January and February.

We have published further information below about what these new restrictions will mean for you.

For the latest Scottish Government advice, please visit:

  • Stay at home guidance
  • Student Information Scotland

 

International travel

From 4am GMT on Monday 18th January, passengers travelling to Scotland from outside the Common Travel Area must have a valid negative Covid-19 test result, taken no more than three days before the scheduled time of departure. Further information can be found on the Scottish Government website. 

Travel restrictions (UPDATED: 15 January)

Travel between Scotland and the rest of the UK is not legal other than for specific exempt purposes (such as education).

In line with Scottish Government guidance, UG and PGT students should not return to their term-time accommodation until such time as on-campus teaching resumes, other than in specific special circumstances. We will continue to support those students who are currently staying in our University accommodation.

If, for essential purposes, you must return to campus, please follow Scottish travel guidance as well as travel guidance for other areas of the UK or any country you will be travelling from.

Travel for non-essential reasons

Despite clear instruction from the Scottish Government, we are aware that some students are still planning to come back to Edinburgh even though their on-campus teaching elements won’t be happening for some time. Please help us to keep everyone safe and help stop the spread of the virus by not returning until you’ve been given a date by your School which confirms when they are planning to resume campus teaching.

We will shortly be asking students to notify us before you travel and to let us know why you think you should be able to travel back to Edinburgh under the current restrictions. If you do return to campus and if you have no scheduled teaching or no other extenuating circumstances, then you will potentially be in breach of the law. Details of this process will be circulated shortly and I really appeal to you all to work with us on this. If you think you must travel in the next few days, please seek advice for now from the Covid Contact Centre or call our Hotline on +44 (0)131 370 0494.

Returning to campus

Phased return in January (UPDATED: 8 January)

Below we have outlined some key points about what the new Scottish Government restrictions will mean for you:

Undergraduate and Postgraduate Taught student

  • As planned, all learning and teaching will restart on programmes of study during the week beginning 11th January 2021 digitally;
  • Undergraduate and postgraduate students who were due to resume in-person, on-campus elements in the week commencing 4th and 11th January and had been contacted by their Schools can do so;
  • However, there is now a delay for all other undergraduate and postgraduate taught students who were due to resume the in-person, on-campus elements of their programme on or after 25th January 2021. Learning and teaching for this group of students will remain digital until further notice and in the vast majority of cases we expect that to be throughout January and February at least.

In line with Scottish Government guidance, UG and PGT students should not return to their term-time accommodation until such time as on-campus teaching resumes, other than in specific special circumstances.

Please be assured that you will continue to receive an excellent standard of digital education throughout this period. Your mental health and wellbeing is an absolute priority, so we will continue to support you as much as possible, no matter where you are currently living.

Postgraduate research

The new restrictions do not alter the information we are providing to postgraduate research students and essential research work may continue in our laboratories and other research facilities.

Please speak with your supervisor for further information on how these new restrictions may impact your work.

Postgraduate students  (UPDATED: 13 January)

Postgraduate taught programmes will restart as planned on 11 January digitally.

Students who were due to resume in-person, on-campus elements in the week commencing 11th January and had been contacted by their Schools can do so.

There is a delay to all those who were due to start the in-person, on-campus elements of their programme on or after 25th January 2021. Learning and teaching for this group of students will remain digital until further notice and in the vast majority of cases we expect that to be throughout January and February at least.

Please note this applies to on-campus postgraduate programmes only, with online postgraduate programmes continuing as planned.

If you are an international postgraduate taught student who has not yet arrived in the UK, please check with your School in advance to confirm when your course will start and when you should arrive on campus. In line with Scottish Government guidance, UG and PGT students should not return to their term-time accommodation until such time as on-campus teaching resumes, other than in specific special circumstances. Students who may need the dates on existing CAS or visas changed should contact studentimmigration@ed.ac.uk for help.

The new guidance does not materially alter the information we are providing to postgraduate research students or research staff and essential research work may continue in our laboratories and other research facilities.

Please speak with your supervisor for further information on how these new restrictions may impact your work.

If you are travelling to Edinburgh from elsewhere in the UK, we strongly advise you to book an asymptomatic Covid-19 test as close to your return date as possible.

Book a test

Please be aware that the asymptomatic Covid-19 testing programme is not sufficient evidence to bypass self-isolation requirements if you are returning to Edinburgh from overseas. From 4am GMT on Monday 18th January, passengers travelling to Scotland from outside the Common Travel Area must have a valid negative Covid-19 test result, taken no more than three days before the scheduled time of departure. Further information can be found on the Scottish Government website.

Covid-19 testing (UPDATED: 15 January)

For students who are resuming in-person teaching after the winter break, we will be offering asymptomatic Covid-19 testing from Thursday 7 January – Friday 12 February 2021.

The tests are voluntary and you do not need to have been tested in order to attend in-person teaching. But we strongly encourage you to book a test if you are returning to Edinburgh from another part of the UK. This will reduce the risk of you passing Covid-19 onto others when you and/or your housemates return to Edinburgh. 

Book a test

You should book a test as close to your return as possible - please remember that you must make two separate bookings using this form. If you are an undergraduate student, you should book your tests to coincide with the date you return to your term time accommodation. Your school should have been in contact with you regarding this.

Please be aware that the asymptomatic Covid-19 testing programme is not sufficient evidence to bypass self-isolation requirements if you are returning to Edinburgh from overseas. From 4am GMT on Monday 18th January, passengers travelling to Scotland from outside the Common Travel Area must have a valid negative Covid-19 test result, taken no more than three days before the scheduled time of departure. Further information can be found on the Scottish Government website.

Social interaction

All students are also being asked to voluntarily restrict their social interactions for 10 days before they return to University and for the same period once they arrive. 

When you arrive back at University, you should also minimise your social mixing for 10 days. This is to help minimise the transmission of the virus.

Quarantine and self-isolation on your return to Edinburgh (UPDATED: 15 January)

From 4am GMT on Monday 18th January, passengers travelling to Scotland from outside the Common Travel Area must have a valid negative Covid-19 test result, taken no more than three days before the scheduled time of departure.  This will be checked by operators, and passengers with a positive test result or an invalid certificate will be refused boarding. 

This pre-departure testing (PDT) does not replace the requirement for all passengers arriving from countries not on the quarantine exemption list to self-isolate for ten days, even with a negative test. 

Further information can be found on the Scottish Government website.

If you need to self-isolate, please follow the usual self-isolation reporting process and inform the University once you are self-isolating in Edinburgh:

Inform the University if you are self-isolating

Support will continue for any student who needs to quarantine and for any students who may need to self-isolate.

University accommodation and rent pauses (UPDATED: 18 January)

If you are an Undergraduate or Postgraduate Taught student staying in University of Edinburgh accommodation and if you are not currently living there, you won’t need to pay rent from 11 January 2021 until either the date you return or the date that your in-person teaching begins.

Who can apply for a rent pause?

  • If you are an Undergraduate or Postgraduate Taught student staying in University of Edinburgh accommodation, and
  • If you are not currently living in your University accommodation (i.e. you haven’t returned to your term-time accommodation following the winter break). Please note that if you returned to your University-managed accommodation, but have no in-person, on-campus teaching, you will have to pay full accommodation fees.

Find out how you can apply for a rent pause

If you have already applied for a rent pause and your teaching dates have changed, please can you email accom.allocations@ed.ac.uk with your student number and new dates. You do not need to email if your arrival dates have not changed.

Leaving University-managed accommodation permanently

If you wish to permanently leave University accommodation, we will be flexible and will not hold you to the 28-day notice period. If you would like to give notice and leave your accommodation please log into MyEd - https://www.myed.ed.ac.uk.

If you have not yet removed your possessions, please be aware students should not return to accommodation on a temporary basis to collect belongings, since this is deemed to be “non-essential” travel. Once travel restrictions are relaxed, we will contact you to provide suitable arrangements for you to collect your belongings.

Private accommodation

Students in privately managed University accommodation (such as Unite) or other private arrangements will need to contact their accommodation provider or landlord.

Advice and support

Information for those shielding (NEW: 8 January)

The UK government has identified two distinct groups who are more vulnerable to the effects of Covid-19.

Further information on Covid-19 and vulnerable groups, including the medical conditions and other factors within the two vulnerable groups, is available on NHS Inform.

If you believe you are at  high risk of severe illness from Covid-19 but do not have a shielding letter, see below, you are advised to discuss your concerns directly with management (staff) or your School (students) in the first instance to consider adjustments to allow you to work from home in the first instance or that may be made to support your return to work/campus, these may be part of Equality/disability considerations.

I have been shielding (extremely high risk)

If you are considered to be at extremely high risk of severe illness from Covid-19 you should have received a letter from NHS Scotland or your GP advising you of this.

The Chief Medical Officer wrote to everyone on the shielding list in the week beginning 4 January to set out advice on the stay at home regulations which came into effect on 5th January 2021.

Any students who are shielding should continue to study from home and not attend classes or placements in person. More information for students with shielded status can be found here.

Further advice can be found on the Scottish Government website:

Changes to shielding advice

If you have an underlying health condition or take regular prescription medicines, but you remain unsure whether you fall into one of the more vulnerable groups, you should contact your own GP practice for advice. 

Further information on Covid-19 and vulnerable groups is available on the NHS Inform webpages.

Accessing support and using our facilities in Semester 2 (UPDATED: 5 January)

Our student support services will continue to operate – whether you are on-campus or not.

Due to the Scottish Government restrictions announced on 4 January, the Student Villages and University-managed cafes are currently closed. They will re-open subject to Government guidance.

Library and study space

Library and study facilities will remain open with existing health and safety measures in place.

Due to the latest Scottish Government restrictions, browsing access and book borrowing of the library collections is suspended. We are developing a new Scan & Deliver service which will provide some access to our print collections, and more information will be provided as this becomes available.&nbsp;

The Centre for Research Collections (CRC) will remain open.

Sport and exercise

The Pleasance Gym will be closed to members until at least Sunday 17 January 2021.
