Information about Semester 2

The latest information and guidance about our Semester Two plans, returning to Edinburgh in January and accessing support.

To find out more about The University's support and guidance for Semester Two, visit our student webpages:

Information for students

Latest Scottish Government guidance 

On Saturday 19 December 2020 the Scottish Government announced further restrictions that are being brought in across Scotland following the emergence of a new, highly contagious strain of the Coronavirus.

From Boxing Day, all of Scotland will have Level 4 restrictions applied, including the closure of non-essential retail and hospitality. The only exceptions will be Orkney, Shetland and the Western Isles, and the other island communities where restrictions have been reduced in recent weeks, who will be placed in Level 3.

For the latest Scottish Government advice, please visit their Student Information website:

Student Information Scotland

Travel restrictions (UPDATED: 23 December)

As set out in the Scottish Government guidelines below, the safest way to spend Christmas is to stay in your own household, in your own home and your own local area.

Please note that students are still be able to travel home from their term-time address and join one other household (for example, your family home) at the end of Semester for the duration of the winter break. The relaxation of the gatherings rules and travel restrictions for students to be able to form an end of term household is not impacted by the changes to the Christmas bubble rules and the festive period guidance announced on 19 December.

Scottish Government guidance for students going home for the winter break

From Boxing Day, in line with existing law, travel between Scotland and the rest of the UK, or across local authority boundaries between areas in Level 3 or 4, will not be legal other than for specific exempt purposes (such as education). Students who have already travelled to their family homes elsewhere in the UK following our Asymptomatic Testing programme should remain there until the date of their planned return to term-time accommodation (please see our returning to campus FAQs below).

If you are choosing to stay at your term-time address, please familiarise yourself with the new Christmas bubble rules which state that you may only form a bubble with another household on Christmas Day only.

We continue to support those students who are staying in University accommodation over the winter break. We are aware that some EU/international students will be unable to travel due to the new restrictions and we will of course support these students. If you are affected, please contact the Covid-19 hotline at +44 (0)131 370 0494.

Returning to campus

Phased return in January (UPDATED: 22 December)

We are reviewing the implications of the recent Government announcements for Semester 2 return. The information below is subject to review.

In January 2021 there will be a phased return to on-campus, in-person teaching for our undergraduate taught students. Students will be asked to return in four phases which will take place over the weeks covering 4th January to 12th February.

This approach will start for a small number of students on 4th January 2021 and build up over four further phases: week beginning 18th January; w/b 25th January; w/b 1st February and w/b 8th February. 

The first phase will prioritise students who are starting professional or clinical placements in early January.

Your school will be in touch with you directly with further details about which students should return in each phase.

Please note that i​​​​nternational students (postgraduate and undergraduate) who have not yet arrived in the UK should continue with their existing plans to arrive when they have secured their visa and made travel arrangements. Students who may need the dates on existing CAS or visas changed should contact studentimmigration@ed.ac.uk for help.

Please read the Scottish Government guidance for students going home for the winter break

Postgraduate students  (UPDATED: 22 December)

Postgraduate taught programmes will restart during week beginning 11th January through our existing hybrid model. We are working through the implications of the latest government guidance with Schools and will be back in contact with you as soon as possible.

The new guidance does not materially alter the information we are providing to postgraduate research students or research staff. Please note that essential on-campus work specified under Level 4 guidance includes all our research unless stipulated otherwise by the Head of College.

Please note that international postgraduate students who have not yet arrived in the UK should continue with their existing plans to arrive when they have secured their visa and made travel arrangements. Students who may need the dates on existing CAS or visas changed should contact studentimmigration@ed.ac.uk for help.

Travelling from another location in the UK back to Edinburgh (UPDATED: 22 December)

From Boxing Day, in line with existing law, travel between Scotland and the rest of the UK, or across local authority boundaries between areas in Level 3 or 4, will not be legal other than for specific exempt purposes.

Travel for education is an exemption to travel restrictions and applies to both domestic and international travel. You can return to University by private, family vehicle if you would prefer to avoid public transport.

You should not share a car with anyone outside of your household, extended household or end of term household to minimise risks of transmission, unless there is no reasonable alternative.

If you choose to return using public transport, you should follow our guidance on how to travel safely. This includes wearing a face covering unless you are exempt, planning your journey in advance to avoid busy times and routes, washing/ sanitising your hands regularly, and keeping your distance while travelling where possible.

Covid-19 testing (UPDATED: 16 December)

To support the safe return of our students to our campuses after the winter break, we will be offering asymptomatic Covid-19 testing from Thursday 7 January – Friday 12 February 2021.

The tests are voluntary and you do not need to have been tested in order to attend in-person teaching. But we strongly encourage you to book a test if you are returning to Edinburgh from another part of the UK. This will reduce the risk of you passing Covid-19 onto others when you and/or your housemates return to Edinburgh. 

Book a test

You should book a test as close to your return as possible - please remember that you must make two separate bookings using this form. If you are an undergraduate student, you should book your tests to coincide with the date you return to your term time accommodation. Your school should have been in contact with you regarding this.

Social interaction

All students are also being asked to voluntarily restrict their social interactions for 10 days before they return to University and for the same period once they arrive. 

When you arrive at University, you should also minimise your social mixing for 10 days. This is to help minimise the transmission of the virus.

International travel to Edinburgh (UPDATED: 22 December)

International students will be able to return to Scotland as they have originally planned. You should check with your School in advance to confirm when your course will start and when you should arrive on campus.

If you are staying in University-managed accommodation, please let us know so that we can support you when you arrive.

Please note that the asymptomatic Covid-19 testing programme is not sufficient evidence to bypass self-isolation requirements if you are returning to Edinburgh from overseas.

You must follow the guidance on international travel and quarantine as appropriate when arriving back in Scotland.

Please read the Government’s International travel and quarantine guidance for the latest information on what you will need to do when entering the UK from another country:

  • International travel and quarantine guidance
  • Country exemptions and information on the process for people entering the UK

We are aware that some EU/international students will be unable to travel due to the new restrictions and we will of course support these students. If you are affected, please contact the Covid-19 hotline at +44 (0)131 370 0494.

Quarantine and self-isolation after the winter break

If you need to quarantine on your return to Edinburgh (e.g. travelling back from a country not on the UK’s self-isolation exemption list), you will need to follow the usual self-isolation reporting process and inform the University once you are self-isolating in Edinburgh:

Inform the University if you are self-isolating

Support will continue for any student who needs to quarantine and for any students who may need to self-isolate.

Accommodation

Returning to your accommodation in Edinburgh

We are strongly encouraging all our undergraduate students, wherever possible, not to return to their term time accommodation until the date of their phased return. We recognise that some undergraduate students may need to return to campus earlier due to personal circumstances, such as employment purposes or health and wellbeing reasons. We will work to support those students on-campus.

Returning or new undergraduate students planning to travel to Scotland from overseas in January can arrive at any time but must follow the guidance on international travel including quarantining if they are from a non-exempt country. 

International students may return to accommodation in the new term as they originally planned and Schools will advise them about the wellbeing support available to them.

University accommodation (UPDATED: 18 December)

Students in university-managed accommodation will not be charged rent in the period from 11th January to their actual arrival date or the date of their phased return (whichever is earlier).

Who can apply for a rent pause?

  • If you are an Undergraduate student staying in University of Edinburgh accommodation, and
  • If you are leaving Edinburgh over the festive break and plan to return in line with Scottish Government / University guidance.

How to apply for a rent pause?

You can apply between the 18th December and 29th January 2021. Please follow the steps below to apply:

  1. Access your MyEd student portal via https://www.myed.ed.ac.uk
  2. Enter your username and password to login
  3. Click on the 'Student Life' tab and select 'Accommodation'
  4. Click 'Launch Services'
  5. Expand 'Term Time 2020 - 21'
  6. Click the ‘Special Request' button under the 'While You're Here' column
  7. Complete the information request.

Once you have completed your information you will receive a confirmation email with more detail on what will happen next.

When will you receive your rebate?

  • If you are paying by monthly instalments the rebate will be applied to your March accommodation payment
  • If you are paying by semester instalments the rebate will be applied to your April accommodation payment
  • If you have paid a full amount, you will receive a refund by end of April.

We do recognise many students won’t be leaving Edinburgh over the winter period. If you are a student who is staying in University of Edinburgh accommodation over the festive period or returning before 11 January 2021 you will not be eligible to receive this rent pause and your rent will be collected in the usual way. Information on winter break support and activities can be accessed here. 

Private accommodation

Students in privately managed University accommodation (such as Unite) or other private arrangements will need to contact their accommodation provider or landlord.

Learning and teaching

Important dates

Learning and teaching will begin in your programme of study in the week commencing 11th January 2021.

There is no change to the Semester end dates or Semester Two examination dates.

Hybrid learning

All teaching will start as planned in week commencing 11th January 2021. However, if you are in a cohort that is not physically on-campus at that time, all of your teaching in the period between 11th January and your scheduled return date will be delivered fully online.

Once your cohort is brought back onto campus, teaching will switch back to the hybrid mode (the mix of on-campus and online learning, similar to Semester 1).
